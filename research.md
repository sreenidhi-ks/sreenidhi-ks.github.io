## Research Overview

My research focuses on understanding lithospheric structure and deformation in plume-influenced and extensional tectonic environments using geophysical observations, numerical modelling, and computational approaches.

---

## Plume–Lithosphere Interaction

I investigate how mantle plumes interact with the lithosphere and influence the evolution of rift systems and plate boundaries.  
My work particularly focuses on the Afar Triple Junction and the Red Sea region, where plume–lithosphere interactions play a major role in controlling rift development and lithospheric deformation.

---

## Lithospheric Strength and Elastic Thickness

A key aspect of my research is estimating the effective elastic thickness (Te) of the lithosphere to understand lithospheric strength and mechanical behaviour.  
I use gravity and geoid data, spectral methods, and joint admittance–coherence approaches to constrain lithospheric structure and weakening processes.

---

## Rift Dynamics and Mid-Ocean Ridge Processes

I study tectono-magmatic processes along mid-ocean ridges and continental rifts, including the Carlsberg Ridge, Central Indian Ridge, and Red Sea Rift system.  
This work combines satellite-derived gravity, bathymetry, and geophysical modelling to understand spreading processes and ridge segmentation.

---

## Computational Geodynamics

My recent work increasingly focuses on numerical modelling and data-driven approaches to understand mantle flow, plume dynamics, and lithospheric deformation.  
I use computational geodynamics tools together with geophysical observations to explore plume–ridge interactions and lithospheric evolution.
