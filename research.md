My research focuses on understanding lithospheric structure, rifting-spreading processes, and mantle dynamics in plume-influenced tectonic environments. I combine geophysical observations, gravity data analysis, and computational geodynamics to investigate how mantle processes influence lithospheric deformation and plate boundary evolution.

---

## Afar Plume and Afar Triple Junction

The Afar region represents one of the most prominent plume-influenced triple junctions on Earth, where the Red Sea, Gulf of Aden, and East African rifts meet. This theme focuses on the tectonic evolution and lithospheric structure of the Afar Triple Junction and the role of mantle plume activity in shaping rifting processes in the region.

---

## Red Sea Rift Evolution

The Red Sea provides an important natural setting to study the transition from continental rifting to oceanic spreading. Work in this theme focuses on lithospheric structure, segmentation, and extensional styles along the Red Sea rift using gravity data and geophysical modelling.

---

## Plume–Mid-Ocean Ridge Interaction

Mantle plumes can influence nearby spreading ridges through thermal and dynamic interactions. This theme examines plume–ridge interaction in the Indian Ocean and its implications for ridge segmentation, spreading processes, and lithospheric structure.

---

## High-Resolution Morphotectonics of Mid-Ocean Ridges

Mid-ocean ridges display complex tectono-magmatic features that can be resolved using high-resolution bathymetric and geophysical datasets. This theme focuses on detailed morphotectonic analysis to understand ridge segmentation, fault development, and seafloor morphology.

---

## Plume–Lithosphere Interaction and Lateral Plume Flow

Mantle plume material can spread laterally beneath the lithosphere and influence tectonic processes far from the plume centre. This theme focuses on understanding lateral mantle plume flow and its effects on lithospheric deformation, rifting, and plume-driven tectonics.
